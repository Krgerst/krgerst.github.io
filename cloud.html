Cloud Computing

"Cloud"

In one of our lectures we discussed cloud storage and how it has been such a huge impact on the way computing happens in todays world. Before cloud computing, in order to host a website, the purchase of servers was required for hosting which made the process not only expensive but also tedious. The more traffic you had, the more servers you would need, plus you would typically need to monitor and maintaining these servers or hire someone else to do the job which could get costly. And just because you had a website, doesn’t mean that there were always high volumes of traffic, so many times these servers would be left idle. With the emergence of “Clouds” people no longer need to buy servers in order to store large amounts of data and can instead buy only what they can use. “Clouds” are just large data centers which have many servers that are able to store data and host websites or applications. These “Clouds” grant the public access to their servers and act as providers by taking care of much of the servers maintenance and security.
